Monitor Resolution!

(A monitor is the screen)

Monitors can affect the performance of a computer if the resolution* is too large for the computer to handle.

When the Monitor Resolution is higher, the quality looks better, but is more difficult for the computer to work with.

*A resolution is the amount of pixels on the screen. Resolutions come in sizes. The more pixels, the more performance will be needed. Here are some examples of monitor resolutions (720p, 1080p, 1440p). The largest one (1440p) will look good, but be harder for the computer to handle, while the lower resolution (720p) will be easier resulting in faster performance, but with the loss of some quality.

Frame Rate

Framerate is the amount of images showed on the screen per second.

A frame is a bit like a picture. When in a video, the frames are playing very quickly, so when you watch the footage it doesn't look like a slideshow.

Imagine that you are recording something on a camera. When you are recording, lots of photos are taken each second. This then makes up a video. Usually videos are recorded at 30fps (Frames per second), so this means that 30 pictures are taken each second making a smooth video, but for instance if the camera was recording at 10fps (Frames per second). the video would be a lot more stutter. I will show a video below.

FPS stands for Frames Per Second. So the higher the FPS, the more frames can fit into a second, making the video smoother as the frames dont need to be streched to fill the second.

10 fps

Only 10 frames are being captured each second, so the frames are being streched to fill up a second therefore the video is stuttery.

30 fps

30 frames are being taken this time, meaning alot more frames to fill the second, making the video smoother.
